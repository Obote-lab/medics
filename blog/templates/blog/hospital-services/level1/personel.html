  • Personnel Requirements for Level 1 Hospitals
  • Medical Officers - Qualified doctors responsible for patient care, diagnosis, and treatment.
  • Nurses - Registered nurses and nursing assistants for bedside care, medication administration, and patient support.
  • Pharmacists - Licensed pharmacists to manage the pharmacy, dispense medications, and provide drug information.
  • Laboratory Technicians - Skilled technicians to perform diagnostic tests and analyze specimens.
  • Radiographers - Trained professionals to operate radiology equipment and perform X-ray procedures.
  • Midwives - Certified midwives to provide prenatal, intrapartum, and postpartum care to pregnant women.
  • Surgeons - Experienced surgeons capable of performing minor surgical procedures.
  • Anesthetists - Certified anesthetists to administer anesthesia during surgical procedures.
  • Administrative Staff - Managers, administrators, and clerical staff to oversee hospital operations, billing, and record-keeping.
  • Maintenance Staff - Technicians and support staff to maintain hospital facilities, equipment, and infrastructure.
  • Support Staff - Housekeeping, security, and catering staff to ensure a clean, safe, and comfortable environment for patients and visitors.
  • Medical Records Officers - Professionals responsible for managing patient records, documentation, and information systems.
  • Public Health Officers - Public health specialists to implement disease prevention and health promotion programs.
  • Health Educators - Educators to provide health education and counseling to patients and the community.
  • Ambulance Drivers - Trained drivers to operate emergency vehicles and transport patients safely.
  • Security Personnel - Security guards to maintain safety and order within the hospital premises.
  • Community Health Workers - Outreach workers to promote health awareness and deliver basic healthcare services in the community.
  • Volunteers - Dedicated individuals offering their time and services to support hospital activities and patient care.
